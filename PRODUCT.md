# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Privacy-conscious people who share links — on social media, in messages, or in email. They want the links they share to be tidy and not leak tracking data to intermediaries. Their context is fast: they have a URL copied, they want a clean short link in seconds, and they want to move on.

## Product Purpose

Crisp takes a messy URL laden with tracking parameters, strips the junk, and returns a deterministic short link that 302-redirects to the sanitized original. No accounts, no dashboards, no analytics. One job: paste, clean, copy, share. Success is a link that looks as clean as the intent behind sharing it.

## Brand Personality

Clean. Swift. No-nonsense.

Crisp is the tool you use because you respect the people you share links with. It doesn't track you, doesn't upsell you, doesn't ask for anything. It does one thing and does it fast.

- **Voice:** Direct and plain. No jargon, no marketing spin, no cute metaphors.
- **Tone:** Quietly confident. It knows what it does and doesn't explain itself.

## Anti-references

- Bloated SaaS landing pages with feature grids, pricing tables, and "request demo" buttons
- Link shorteners that harvest click data and sell analytics
- Tools that require account creation for basic functionality
- Dashboards, notifications, onboarding flows — anything that gets between the user and the one action they came for

## Design Principles

1. **One thing, one move.** Every screen serves exactly one user action. If a page has more than one input, more than one button, or more than one reason to exist, it's wrong.
2. **Show the work.** When the tool acts on the user's data (stripping params, generating a code), show exactly what happened and what changed. Transparency is the product.
3. **Disappear when done.** The interface steps out of the way the moment the user has what they need. No confirmation modals, no "share on Twitter" prompts, no follow-up upsells.
4. **Privacy by construction.** No accounts, no logs of who submitted what, no cookies for tracking. The tool is the product; the user's data is not.
5. **Deterministic trust.** The same URL always produces the same short link. Predictability communicates reliability.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.2 AA minimum.
- Full keyboard navigation for the single action flow.
- Color is never the sole carrier of information (stripped params shown as text labels, not just badge colors).
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Dark mode as a first-class experience, not an afterthought.
